The Wallet on the Sidewalk

A web-based non-linear story about honesty, pressure, and the choices people make when nobody seems to be watching.

Start Story Story Description Narrative Map

Story Description

This interactive story follows a student walking home from school who finds a wallet on the sidewalk. Inside the wallet is $100, an ID card, and a phone number written on a small piece of paper. The story takes place around a school neighborhood, including the sidewalk, the school hallway, a possible meeting spot, and a police station.

The user must decide what to do with the wallet. They can call the number, bring the wallet to the police, keep the money, return it later, or pretend they never found it. The story has several endings, including a good ending, a neutral ending, and a bad ending. The purpose of the story is to show how one small decision can lead to different consequences depending on whether the character chooses honesty, fear, or selfishness.

Narrative Map

Start: You find a wallet on the sidewalk after school.

  • Choice A: Call the phone number.
    • Meet the owner in a public place → Good Ending
    • Bring the wallet to the police instead → Good Ending
    • Hang up and feel guilty later → Neutral or Bad Ending
  • Choice B: Bring the wallet to the police station.
    • Police contact the owner → Good Ending
  • Choice C: Keep the money.
    • Friend asks about the wallet → Return it and apologize → Neutral Ending
    • Friend asks about the wallet → Pretend you never saw it → Bad Ending
    • Spend the money → Admit the truth or get caught → Neutral or Bad Ending
  • Choice D: Take it to the school office.
    • School contacts the owner → Good Ending
    • Take the money first → Bad Ending
